Who I am

I'm an early-career cybersecurity practitioner focused on defensive security. I got into the field the hands-on way: spinning up my own lab, generating attacks against it, and learning to see them from the other side of the SIEM.

With 5 years of experience as a software developer helping businesses establish and grow their online presence, I've built real-world solutions using Python, JavaScript, Java, and Spring Boot. Over those years I developed a deep appreciation for how systems are built and, just as importantly, how they can be exposed. That understanding is what drew me to cybersecurity. I'm now ready to pivot into the field, and I've been able to build hands-on projects that demonstrate my capability, including a SOC home lab where I simulate attacks, forward telemetry into a SIEM, and engineer detections to catch them, and a security operations toolkit that automates routine analyst tasks. My development background allows me to go beyond simply operating security tools; I can understand the code behind them and build my own. I'm seeking an opportunity to begin my cybersecurity career as a SOC Analyst, IAM Analyst, or Security Engineer, where I can apply a developer's technical foundation alongside a defender's mindset to help protect the systems businesses rely on.

Outside the console I'm curious, detail-obsessed, and a little bit extra about clean dashboards and color-coded notes. Cyber, but make it me. 💽

Roles I'm interested in

🛡️

SOC Analyst

Monitoring, triggering, and investigating security alerts. Correlating logs across a SIEM, mapping activity to MITRE ATT&CK, escalating real threats, and writing clear incident notes, the frontline of detection & response.

⚙️

Security Engineer

Managing digital identities and enforcing least privilege — provisioning and deprovisioning accounts, designing role-based access (RBAC), running access reviews, and configuring SSO and MFA. Making sure the right people have the right access, and no one has more than they need.

🔑

IAM Analyst / Engineer

Building and hardening the tooling that keeps defenders effective, deploying detection rules, automating alert triage, scripting response playbooks, and standing up monitoring infrastructure that scales.

SOC Home Lab

A self-built security operations lab where I simulate attacks and practice detecting them end-to-end. I generated adversary activity against virtual endpoints, shipped the telemetry into a SIEM, and built the detections and dashboards to catch it.

  • Deployed a virtualized environment (Windows + Linux endpoints) with centralized log collection.
  • Instrumented hosts with Sysmon and forwarded events into a SIEM for correlation.
  • Ran simulated attacks (recon, brute force, lateral movement) and mapped them to MITRE ATT&CK.
  • Wrote custom detection rules and dashboards, then documented each investigation as a repeatable playbook.
